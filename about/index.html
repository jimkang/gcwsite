About

Ghost Crab Workshop makes two kinds of software:


  • Software that makes lives easier.
  • Software that expresses an idea or feeling.

In both cases, Ghost Crab Workshop prioritizes consistency and reliability, both in functionality and design.



Ghost Crab Workshop has one employee. My name is Jim Kang. I enjoy software, but I also enjoy board games, podcasts, mixed martial arts, composing music, and carnitas. I live in Cambridge, MA with my wife and cat, who are both pretty cool.


In addition to Ghost Crab Workshop's apps, I've worked on the teams at Modo Labs that were behind the Harvard, Technology Review, and Harvard Reunions iOS apps. Before getting into iOS development, I worked in web development. Before that, I spent several years as a native Windows developer. We're at a rather exciting time for technology and independent software making, and I'm glad to be here.



Ghost Crab Workshop may be available for consulting on your iOS project.


I have done a lot of work with Cocoa Touch, Objective-C, and REST-based web services in the last few years. I have been through the entire development cycle - from requirements gathering and design to deployment and maintenance - several times. And I'm not afraid of Photoshop.
